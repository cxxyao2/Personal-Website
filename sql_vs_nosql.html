CAP theorem:

For distributed computing system, it is impossible to satisfy 3 requirements at the same time:

  • Consistency
  • Aavailability
  • Partition tolerance
Highly scalable.Efficient.Available.
    High Availability:
  1. If a database is available,the users of its data --that is , applications, customers, and business users can access it. HA aims to ensure an agreed level of operational performance
    Key Principles:
  1. Eliminate any single point of failure: Adding redundancy, so that the failure of one part does not lead to the collapse of the entire system
  2. Reliable crossover: in a redundant system, the crossover point itself becomes a single point of failure.Fault-tolerant systems must provide a reliable crossover or automatic switchover mechanism to avoid failure.
  3. Detection of failures:If the above two principles are proactively monitored, the user never sees a system failure.
ACID Principles
  • Atomicity: an indivisible and irreducible series of database operations such that either all occur, or nothing occurs. eg. 1) withdraw $10 from A account 2) save $10 to B account. 1), 2) all are committed or rollback.
  • Consistency:Data written to DB must be valid according to all defined rules,including constraints, trigger, cascades, and other combinations thereof2.
  • Isolation: parallel transactions are isolated. B transaction cannot see the dirty data(not committed) of A transaction
  • Durability: data persistance after server is shutdown
